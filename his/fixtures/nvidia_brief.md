# NVIDIA — internal notes (synthesized, not confidential)

NVIDIA Corporation designs GPUs, SoCs, and AI accelerator systems. Founded
1993; headquartered Santa Clara. Public on NASDAQ as NVDA.

Strategic posture (2025–2026, public-source synthesis):

- AI infrastructure is the dominant revenue line — Hopper and Blackwell
  generations drive datacenter sales to hyperscalers and sovereign-AI
  programs.
- CUDA ecosystem is the moat. Decades of developer mindshare and library
  fragmentation make swap-out costs prohibitive for incumbent workloads.
- Margin expansion narrative pinned to networking (Mellanox/InfiniBand)
  and full-stack systems (DGX), not just silicon.
- Headwinds: export controls to China (H20/L20 SKU gymnastics), supply
  concentration at TSMC, and the emerging "post-transformer" architecture
  bets that could relativize raw GPU FLOPs as the bottleneck.

The pitch deck tells a story of "every company becomes an AI factory." The
public filings tell a more concentrated story — three hyperscalers do
most of the buying, and the long tail of enterprise AI is still
experimentation budget, not P&L line items.
